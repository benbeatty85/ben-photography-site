Amalfi Coast

We had a difficult time in the Amalfi Coast, I had been pick pocketed while waiting for the bus. On top of that, it rained for eight of the ten days we were there. Powering through and trying to stay positive, we began to see the beauty of this majestic part of Italy. This is a dream destination, cliff side villages connected by a road with the most beautiful scenery.

Even though we were unlucky, it’s the first place I think about when planning a return visit to Europe, maybe a second chance to eliminate the bitterness and have the Amalfi experience we deserved.

We stayed in the non touristy fishing village of Minori. The landscape of this region is unique and postcard worthy. Lemon groove covered cliffs, dotted with houses that seem to teater along the mountains and the backdrop of the crystal blue Tyrrhenian sea. We stayed in an apartment built in the 17th century, which was right next door to a 1st century Roman ruin.

The star of the show in the Amalfi Coast are the lemons. Trust me when I say this, you have never tasted a lemon in your life until you eat one here, it’s a completely different product. People eat them like apples the fruit is so juicy and sweet.

Tuscany

The most famous region in Italy, Tuscany brings you that quintessential experience. Marissa’s favourite destination, Florence looks like a movie set it’s so perfectly constructed. We have been to Florence twice now and enjoyed it so much, we decided to take our moms on a Tuscan adventure in 2016.

We were very excited to spend time in Tuscany with family. One of the difficulties of travel is attempting to explain to loved ones your unique experiences on the road. Sharing moments in far away destinations allows you to reminisce together and enriches your relationships.

We stayed one night in Pisa, you probably know why. The night we arrived we had a very late dinner. After eating, I suggested we walk to the leaning tower. I couldn’t wait, I was finally here and had to see it. After getting some directions from the locals, we found it. It was around midnight so we were the only ones there, aside from the machine gun equipped guards of course. What an experience it was, for a few minutes we were the only people in the world admiring the Leaning Tower of Pisa.

We also spent a few nights in the countryside town of Greve in Chianti. Our hotel was located in an old, refurbished pasta factory, which, let’s be honest, doesn’t get more Italian than that. We spent the few days hiking the glorious countryside, cooking our own Italian meals and visiting the neighbouring wineries and ancient villages.

Rome

My favourite destination so far has been Italy and more specifically Rome, a city that requires you to travel slowly to enjoy it. I'll never forget my first night. We had just spent hours travelling and decided to pick a random trattoria after settling in. As I ate and looked around, it began to sink in where I was. Rome is one of the most famous cities in the world and for 5 nights it would be our playground.

The history is alive in Rome, not only with it's famous sites but many of the people are living this romantic reality. Marissa and I spent the entire first night just walking around. We stumbled across the Trevi Fountain, the Spanish Steps and had some gelato along the way. It was magical and I'll never forget it for the rest of my life.
