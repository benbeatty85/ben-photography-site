Montreal

We had already lived in Toronto for five years after coming back from Korea. We loved Toronto but wanted a change of scenery. The European way of life and festive nature of Montreal really appealed to us. People seem to be more relaxed here, we think a better work-life balance has something to do with it.

Also, the cost of living is much lower compared with Toronto, giving us more financial freedom to enjoy what Montreal has to offer. We packed our stuff and set out on another adventure, adding Montreal to the list of places we’ve now lived.

Montreal has a certain reputation. When we told people we lived in Toronto, they would feel bad for us. Now that we tell them we live in Montreal, their eyes light up. People really like this city, and for good reason. It’s a very active city, the summer brings endless festivals to attend.

The food scene also has character and is unique to Montreal, I can never go back to grocery store bagels and non Quebec poutine. Montreal also has that European feel in it's architecture, we love just walking though The Plateau or Old Montreal, soaking in the details of the buildings. Montreal will not be home forever, but it will be a very enjoyable stepping stone for us.

Toronto

Toronto gets a bad rap with people who do not live there. They will tell you it’s loud, dirty, has a horrible metro system and constant traffic jams. These are all very true, but there is something about this city that I just love so much. You have to live in Toronto to get it, there is a vibe that is unique.

It was our first home as a married couple, we spent five great years exploring the city and enjoyed it’s open minded attitudes. We discovered so much about the world in Toronto, it being so multi-cultural. It really sparked our passion for travel, getting glimpses of a variety of cultures.

This is what I loved the most about Toronto, other, less tolerant people could learn a great lesson from this city. People from all of the world just living together, it never felt forced or unnatural. It was just the way of life and the food scene benefitted from this.

We were introduced to so many types of food; Ethiopian, Turkish, Pakistani, Sri Lankan, the list goes on and on. If it exists in the world, you can find it in Toronto. We will always miss living there, it was a significant chapter in our lives and we will always feel like Torontonians no matter where we live.
