Bowness-on-Windermere

We had been travelling for two weeks when we arrived in the English country side, this was the first time the sun consistently made an appearance and didn’t look like it was going to rain.

We stayed at a really nice bed and breakfast, owned by a young English couple who just bought the business two weeks prior, they had been tired of the rat race in London and decided to jump into the hospitality industry. Every morning we would gather in their small dining room and enjoy a full English breakfast to start the day.

The scenery surrounding this town was beautiful, rolling hills and I counted more sheep than people. One morning, we embarked on a hike to the cottage of Beatrix Potter, the famous children’s author. This is where she wrote many of her books and most of her illustrations were inspired from either the house or surrounding town.

We also did a boat tour around Lake Windermere, which is the largest lake in England. Passing by many English villages and green landscapes as far as the eye could see.

London

The envy of so many other cities, London just has this vibe to it that impresses you. Similar to my experience living in Toronto, London is multicultural, large and loud. All qualities I seem to enjoy in a city. We were really impressed with the transit in London, “The Tube” takes you anywhere you want to be and runs efficiently. London is one of those iconic cities that has an enormous amount of attractions to take in.

This was the first time during our trip that we wrote out a daily itinerary. We had a great time travelling around the city and finding the hot spots. We walked in the footsteps of The Beatles on Abbey Road, hiked up Primrose Hill, visited some of the most impressive museums we had ever seen, walked across tower bridge and visited the Tower of London. We even did a tour of Shakespeare’s Globe theatre.
