Dublin and Cliffs of Moher

Dublin was the first stop on what was a four month adventure in Europe. We arrived exhausted from our red eye flight, running on only a traveler’s high. We hit the ground running as soon as we settled in with a traditional breakfast for me and a big gulp sized cup of espresso for Marissa.

I bought a rain jacket in Dublin three days in, which explains the kind of weather we were getting. This was a great excuse to visit many of Dublin’s excellent pubs, indulging our taste buds in Ireland’s famous drink, Guinness.

We also took a day trip across the country to visit the Cliffs of Mohar. What almost looked like the end of world, this site is majestic and raw. The wind so strong that day that the bus driver decided to stay on board instead of going for his usual walk along the cliffs.

We feared for our lives, imagining a gust of wind tossing us over the side, delivering us to our impending dooms. We were spared that day, and allowed to soak in the greatness that is the Cliffs of Moher.
