Lagos

We arrived in Lagos curious as to what we were getting ourselves into. We decided to spend almost a third of our European trip in Portugal, not really knowing anything about the culture and sort basing our decision on an episode of No Reservations with Anthony Bourdain in Lisbon.

Portugal was to be our wild card, and it paid off. We stayed in a beach front hotel for a week, giving us a chance for some much needed R & R combined with our usual city exploration.

The beaches in Lagos are the most beautiful and majestic we have ever seen. Cliffs galore and perfect sandy conditions led to amazing hikes and beach lounging. Lagos is also famous for it’s grottos along the shoreline. We decided to do a boat tour that took you in, out and around these grottos.

Our tour guide seemed to speak every language know to mankind and we went out with a terrified couple from Austria in a small dingy boat. The Austrian girl spent half the time crying because she thought she was going to die and our hippie skipper needed to play tour guide and calm her down at the same time, it was entertaining to say the least.

Lisbon

We decided to end our four month long trip in Lisbon, we would stay an entire month and really get to know the city intimately. Lisbon remains one of our favourite destinations, going back with our moms two years after visiting initially. Marissa’s brother even flew into Lisbon to stay with us for a few days during our first trip there.

What an incredible experience this was, Lisbon now feels like a second home to us and it’s a city that we really connected with. From the traditional Fado music, to it’s fresh and simple approach to food and it’s year round ideal weather, it should be on everyone’s travel radar.

We lived like a local for the month, shopping at the neighbourhood grocery store everyday, hanging our laundry outside of our Juliette balcony overlooking traditional Portuguese tiled buildings and dining with the locals. We also happened to be in Lisbon during the 2014 World Cup, which was a really cool experience.

Although Portugal didn’t fair well that year, being in football country during the World Cup just filled the city with so much energy. We were excited that we became so familiar with Lisbon right before it’s tourism started to blow up, giving us a chance to get to know it before it became “cool”.
