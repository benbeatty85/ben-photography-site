Edinburgh

We would like to spend more time in Scotland, Edinburgh left a great impression on us. You can tell there is a pride in preserving the history, a great balance of the old and new. The castle located in the middle of the city is one of the most impressive buildings we had ever seen.

Holyrood Park was a great destination as well, providing the highest point in the city and therefore the best views, when it’s not foggy of course.

Edinburgh is a great destination for supernatural tourism. We did a ghost tour that took you through Mary King's Close, which is a city under modern day Edinburgh. Plague victims in the 17th century would be isolated in these locations, shunned from society.

Old cities like Edinburgh are built upon layers of it’s former self, trapping and preserving history for anyone willing to find it. This close is apparently haunted by a number of ghosts, including a little girl who was believed to have been a plague victim. There is a small room where visitors leave toys and dolls behind for her, as you can imagine it’s a creepy scene.
