Avignon

French culture is worshipped the world over. Their overwhelmingly complicated, amazing approach to gastronomy, the ability to sit in a cafe for hours on end just to watch the world go by and just their joie de vivre really appeal to Marissa and I.

Avignon would be our first stop in France and we were excited. We stayed in a beautiful Airbnb in the heart of town, steps away from the Palais des Papes.

Marissa unfortunately came down with a terrible cold and was out of commission for an entire day. The time we did have together was spent walking through the narrow, ancient streets and having picnic’s along the Rhone river facing the famous Pont d’Avignon.

We were living le joie de vivre and were loving it. We only had a couple of days in Avignon, but our quick visit will not be soon forgotten.

Paris

We have been to Paris twice now, once together and another time Marissa went with her mom and I with my brother. There are a few attractions that you come across in life where you will always remember your first experience, the Eiffel Tower is one of them.

I found myself continually looking back whenever it was in sight, as if it was going somewhere. We all know Paris is fantastic; the food, architecture, it’s cool residents, history, etc. If you need us to explain why you need to visit Paris, then you’re most likely a lost cause anyway.

I fell in love with this city so much that I asked my brother to join me in a gastronomical adventure when I returned from Korea and Marissa did the same with her mother, although their focus was more attraction based and less food. My brother and I would spend a week in the city of lights, eating the best food and drinking the most expensive wine we’d ever had.

We drank at least one bottle of wine and ate a cheese plate everyday. The meals were rich and decadent and we felt like french kings. Our biggest splurge would have been a $75 black truffle omelette at a very old, classic bistro. That was the price for only one, so we had to split it. We left Paris defeated, stomachs lined with butter and cheese, craving the sweet relief of a salad bar.
