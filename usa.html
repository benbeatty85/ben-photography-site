Las Vegas and Grand Canyon

Prior to visiting, Las Vegas came across as this parallel universe to us. A place where people go to let loose, take on a different persona and shave a bit of time off their lives. It’s possible to have this crazy Vegas adventure, but there are many sides to this unique city.

Marissa and I are not huge gamblers, more interested in taking our chances at the infamous, sketchy Vegas buffet rather than trying our luck around the roulette table. Vegas is a great destination for a few days, take in a show or two, eat at the world famous restaurants and enjoy the grandeur of the hotels along the strip.

We also decided to rent a car and drive to the Grand Canyon. Although, a majestic and must see attraction, we underestimated the driving time and weather. It took four hours each way to get there, however, we did enjoy the drive through the Nevada and Arizona desserts. We also stopped at the Hoover Dam along the way, which was very impressive.

It was February, we thought the year round weather in Arizona is always pleasant, we were wrong. As we drove closer to the Grand Canyon, we began to notice the scenery changing. It went from short wearing weather when we left Vegas, to snowing as we approached our destination. Marissa had to buy branded sweatpants from the gift shop when we arrived. Lessons were learned that day.

Los Angeles

The City of Angels, infamous for it’s unfulfilled Hollywood dreams, sandy beaches and In-N-Out burger. When you travel to LA, you are overwhelmed with so much of what the city has to offer. First off, we thought we saw the worst of North American traffic living in Toronto, we were dead wrong.

You have to rent a car in LA and the city suffers from eternal rush hour. You tolerate the traffic because the sights in LA are world famous. We hiked up the Hollywood Hills to the Hollywood sign, took a celebrity bus tour, did a MGM studio tour and other mandatory attractions.

We would like to visit Los Angeles again, maybe take a different approach and see how the locals live. There is much more to LA then the typical sights of Hollywood. We really enjoyed the Venice Beach area, definitely more our speed. It had such a chill vibe and was an excellent place to people watch, the “unique” residents use this boardwalk as their catwalk, showing off their individuality.

The food in LA is outstanding, both on the cutting edge of modern cuisine and more of an authentic approach to Mexican food. The seafood is obviously amazing too, I enjoyed a few fresh fish meals while visiting. LA, like so many cities, seems to be best enjoyed at a slow pace. Getting around is such a pain that bouncing around to various attractions is not an ideal approach.
