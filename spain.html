Barcelona

The culture in Spain is well preserved, the Spanish are a very passionate group, embodying attitudes that go back centuries. Barcelona being our first stop, which has it’s own distinct Catalan culture, was a treat of a destination. We stayed in the less touristy neighbourhood of El Poblenou, living and eating with the locals and doing the whole outside laundry hanging thing.

You really see this passion for life in the architecture in Barcelona, mostly represented by the work of Antoni Gaudí. We got to visit a few of his creations including Park Guell, which is where he lived and is a great way to spend an afternoon with a camera.

The food in Spain, and especially Barcelona is outstanding and fairly affordable compared to other European destinations. The city has one of the most impressive markets in Europe, La Boqueria, which is also a must do for some traditional eats and very colourful food stalls. Even doing groceries in Barcelona was fun.

We were finding amazing, super cheap produce everywhere, ingrediants that were a delight to cook. We spent a lot of time just walking and biking around the city, soaking in the architecture and various flavours it had to offer. We hope to visit Barcelona again one day.

Granada

Our second destination in Spain would be Granada, selected due to it’s history, typically Spanish vibe and world famous Moorish palace, The Alhambra. This was one of my favourite destinations in Europe, I remember walking through the streets of Granada on our first day thinking to myself how lucky we were to have an opportunity to visit places that looked this ancient and well preserved.

It’s one of those cities that put many things into perspective for me. Made me realize why we were putting other life decisions on hold to just explore.

Granada is a very “Spanish” city, where bull fighting still exists and tapas are free when you buy a drink, which is the traditional way of doing it. We attended a Flamenco show that was hosted in a cave, this was a great cultural experience and again, showcased the passion of Spanish culture.

We also visited The Alhambra, which is a must do. It’s the most visited attraction in Spain for good reason. The rooms in this Moorish palace include mind-boggling detail work. Every single inch of the palace was designed or constructed for a reason. You walk through this palace trying not to blink as to not miss anything, there is nothing else like it on Earth. Our Airbnb even had a rooftop patio that overlooked Granada and The Alhambra, needless to say, we spent lot’s of time up there.
